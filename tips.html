Perspective Trick


1. Here's a shortcut to drawing weird exaggerated perspectives. First, draw a VERY quick doodle of what you want to draw in a very simple straight, head-on perspective. Just enough to be able to tell where everything is and what shape it is.

2. Next, draw a box showing the perspective you wish to draw. Basically, the box that would fit around the thing you're drawing, though it doesnt really have to be that close at all.

3. Then, use your drawing program's "Free Transform" tool to fit the original doodle you made to this box, making whatever adjustments you want to make the perspective look nicer.

4. Finally, draw your actual proper sketch over this warped doodle, making sure to give the shapes their proper 3D volume, now that they're all flat. Everything will be in a pretty nice place without all that much pain and you can go on to putting in more details and finishing the drawing.
