Animation terms

Keyframes

keyframes are the frames animators draw first, they establish the basic movment of the animation and help keep everything consistent

In-betweens

in betweens are the frames drawn in between key frames to complete the full animation. the keyframes are used as a base and the inbetweens are drawn in to complete the illusion of movment

Smear frames

these are frames in which the character is distorted to better create the illusion of movment. these frames are only meant to be seen for a second and are usually employed when a charcater is moving very fast

Impact frames

impact frames are frames that happen directly after an impact offten inverting the colour pallete or making everything monochrome. these frames exist to create a sense of punch to a characters actions and are common in fight animation

Tips

Stretch Frames


stretch frames add emphasis to movment, for example if you where moving very fast

Squash Frames

squash frames emphasive impact, they also offten give the object a rubbery quality

Rebound


rebound height is important to keep track of because its a visual indicator of weight. a heavy hard object wont rebound much and will maintain a static shape while doing it. meanwhile a rubber object will squash and the rebound very high

Texture and weight

Every material is different and thus every material moves differently aswell. qualities like weight, density and structure are important to keep in mind when animating. this ensure your drawings not only look like an object but move like it too
